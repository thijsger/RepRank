Reppd — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 June 2026

Reppd is a Garmin Connect IQ app that counts bodyweight exercise reps and keeps an online leaderboard. This policy explains what data the app sends to our server, why, and how you can have it removed. We keep this short on purpose: the app collects as little as possible.

What we collect

  • A display name — the name you type in the app (optional; shown on the leaderboard).
  • A random app ID — generated on your watch to tell users apart. It is not linked to your Garmin account, email, or real identity.
  • Exercise scores — the exercise, the rep count (or plank time), and the date.
  • A short motion sample — accelerometer and gyroscope values recorded only during the set you save. We use this purely to verify the score is a real movement (anti-cheat). It contains no location, heart rate, or personal data.

What we do NOT collect

  • No email address, phone number, or real name (unless you type one).
  • No GPS or location data.
  • No heart rate or other health metrics.
  • No advertising identifiers. We do not use trackers or ads.

How we use it

  • To show weekly and all-time leaderboards.
  • To verify that a submitted score reflects real movement.
  • To show your own rank and progress.

We never sell or share your data with third parties. Data is used only to run the app's features described above.

Where it is stored

Scores are stored on our hosting provider (Render) and database (Neon PostgreSQL), located in the EU. Connections use HTTPS. The motion sample is only used for the anti-cheat check at submission time and is not kept as a long-term profile.

Deleting your data

Because the app uses an anonymous random ID, we cannot identify you personally. To have your name and scores removed, email us with the display name you used and we will delete the matching entries.

Children

Reppd is a general fitness app and is not directed at children under 13.
